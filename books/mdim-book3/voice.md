# VOICE FINGERPRINT: My Dog Is Judging Me — Book 3

## Narrator: Beatrice "Bea" Chen (9 years old)

### Voice Characteristics
- **First-person diary format** — written as if Bea is writing in her detective notebook
- **Age-appropriate vocabulary** — Year 4 level, but with occasional sophisticated words she's picked up from Barnaby
- **Self-deprecating humor** — Bea is aware she's "weird" and leans into it
- **Data-driven observations** — She notices patterns, makes lists, draws charts
- **Doodle descriptions** — Every chapter includes at least one doodle prompt in brackets
- **British English** — The family lives in England (Riverstone)

### Sentence Structure
- Short, punchy sentences for humor
- Longer, more complex sentences when she's analyzing something
- Frequent use of dashes and ellipses for thought fragments
- Occasional direct address to the reader ("If there is one thing you need to know...")

### Dialogue Style
- **Bea**: Casual, modern kid speak. "Okay, so..." "Basically..." "The thing is..."
- **Barnaby**: Formal, intellectual, sarcastic. "I think the word is 'lumpy'." "As usual, we are alone in our brilliance."
- **Maya**: Scientific, precise. "According to my calculations..." "The data suggests..."
- **Lulu**: Simple, chaotic. "I WANT THAT!" "Why? Why? Why?"
- **Simba**: Dramatic, grandiose. "I am the true ruler of Riverstone!" "You meddling children!"
- **Mummy**: Organized, schedule-obsessed. "It's in the Neon Violet block." "Five minutes!"

### Doodle Style
- **Format**: [Doodle: description with caption]
- **Style**: Simple line drawings, Diary of a Wimpy Kid / Dork Diaries aesthetic
- **Content**: Character portraits, maps, charts, diagrams, funny asides
- **Captions**: Short, punchy, often sarcastic
- **Frequency**: At least 1-2 doodles per chapter

### Prose Rules (Anti-Slop)
- NO "delve", "utilize", "leverage", "facilitate"
- NO "a sense of [emotion]" — show it
- NO "the air was thick with" — be specific
- NO "eyes widened" — use specific reactions
- NO "heart pounded in chest" — use specific physical reactions
- NO "raven hair cascaded" — just describe the hair
- Vary sentence length (short for humor, longer for analysis)
- Use British spelling (colour, favourite, programme)
