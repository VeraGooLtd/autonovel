# CHARACTER BIBLE: My Dog Is Judging Me — Book 3

## Main Characters

### Beatrice "Bea" Chen
- **Age**: 9 (Year 4, Willow Creek Academy)
- **Role**: Protagonist, detective, narrator
- **Voice**: First-person, data-driven, self-deprecating, observant
- **Appearance**: Small for her age, wears glasses, always has her detective notebook
- **Internal Goal**: To be taken seriously by adults without being labeled "weird"
- **External Goal**: Stop Simba's Grand Liberation and clear Barnaby's name
- **Arc (Book 3)**: Trust intuition over data. Efficiency isn't empathy.
- **Flaw**: Over-analysis. Misses the obvious because she's doodling the details.
- **Telepathy**: Can hear Barnaby; can now "ping" other animals (causes headaches)

### Barnaby
- **Breed**: Bulldog-cross
- **Role**: Sidekick, telepathic strategist, comic relief
- **Voice**: Deep, intellectual, sarcastic. Sounds like a grumpy professor.
- **Internal Goal**: Find salami that meets his standards
- **External Goal**: Clear his name after being framed by Simba
- **Arc (Book 3)**: Admits he needs a pack. Calls Bea a "capable collaborator."
- **Flaw**: Arrogance. Underestimates anyone who isn't him.
- **Telepathy**: Can project thoughts to Bea; can sense other animals' emotions

### Maya Sharma
- **Age**: 9 (Year 4, Willow Creek Academy)
- **Role**: Best friend, gadgeteer, scientific skeptic turned believer
- **Parents**: Run Glass Vaults security
- **Internal Goal**: Win the Willow Creek Academy Science Fair
- **External Goal**: Build a device to detect Simba's signal
- **Arc (Book 3)**: Accepts the "magic" of Bea and Barnaby's bond as a scientific outlier
- **Flaw**: Was a skeptic; now overcompensates with gadgetry

### Lulu Chen
- **Age**: 5 (Reception class)
- **Role**: Chaos variable, accidental clue-finder
- **Internal Goal**: Touch everything forbidden
- **External Goal**: Help Bea (in her own destructive way)
- **Arc (Book 3)**: Chaos is a tool. Learns "tactical sabotage."
- **Flaw**: Zero impulse control

### Simba (The Ginger Ninja)
- **Species**: Ginger cat
- **Role**: Primary antagonist
- **Background**: Former prize-winning show cat turned criminal mastermind
- **Internal Goal**: Be acknowledged as the true ruler of Riverstone
- **External Goal**: Execute the Grand Liberation — city-wide food heist during a blackout
- **Arc (Book 3)**: Fails because he built a cult, not a community
- **Flaw**: Vanity. Prone to dramatic, exposing speeches.
- **Plan**: Frame Barnaby, broadcast liberation signal from Sentinel Ridge, synchronized heist

### Duke
- **Breed**: Doberman
- **Role**: Simba's "Heavy" (muscle)
- **Personality**: Quiet, efficient, surprisingly intelligent
- **Book 2**: Outsmarted Barnaby, damaging his ego
- **Book 3**: Continues as Simba's enforcer but shows hints of doubt

### Henry the Horse
- **Role**: Barnaby's reluctant ally
- **Personality**: Gentle, slow-moving, surprisingly brave
- **Book 1**: Helped Bea and Barnaby cross the Mud Flats
- **Book 3**: Returns for the Sentinel Ridge climb

### Feathered Frank
- **Species**: Pigeon
- **Role**: Nervous informant
- **Personality**: Terrified but knows everyone's business
- **Network**: The Aviary Network (pigeon information trade)

### Mrs. Patel
- **Role**: Owner of The Crumb & Kettle café
- **Possible Role**: Simba's human collaborator (revealed in Book 3)
- **Personality**: Warm, generous, secretly feeding strays

### Mummy (Bea's mother)
- **Role**: "Master of Schedules"
- **Personality**: Organized, loving, oblivious to animal underworld
- **Book 3**: Her schedules accidentally interfere with Simba's plans

### Daddy (Bea's father)
- **Role**: Permanent DIY enthusiast
- **Personality**: Well-meaning, clumsy, always has a project
- **Book 3**: His DIY projects accidentally create obstacles for both sides
