# CANON: My Dog Is Judging Me — Book 3

## Continuity Facts (from Books 1-2)

### Character Facts
- **Bea is 9** (Year 4, Willow Creek Academy)
- **Barnaby is a bulldog-cross** (exact breed unknown, he doesn't like to discuss it)
- **Maya's parents run Glass Vaults security**
- **Mummy is Chinese-British** (the family name is Chen)
- **Daddy is permanent DIY enthusiast** (projects never finish)
- **Lulu is 5** (Reception class, sticky fingers)

### Telepathy Rules
- Barnaby can project thoughts to Bea (one-way, mostly)
- Bea can now "ping" other animals (caused by evolution in Book 2)
- Pinging gives Bea headaches
- No other human can hear animals
- The bond was caused by a shared electrical shock from an old radio

### World Facts
- Riverstone is an English town
- Highspire Ruins are ancient ruins outside town
- The Glass Vaults is a shopping center
- Animals have their own criminal underworld (the Meow-fia)
- Simba is a ginger cat (The Ginger Ninja)
- Duke is a Doberman (Simba's "Heavy")
- Henry the Horse lives near the Mud Flats
- The Crumb & Kettle café is run by Mrs. Patel

### Plot Facts (from Book 1)
- Book 1: The Salami Saboteur — Simba stole the Grand Prize Fancy Meat Platter
- The salami was "mysteriously" returned by Henry the Horse
- Simba was captured but escaped

### Plot Facts (from Book 2)
- Book 2: The Glass Vaults Gambit — Simba targeted the mall's deli vault
- Duke outsmarted Barnaby (damaged his ego)
- Bea's telepathy evolved (can now ping other animals)
- Maya's "Meow-fia Detector" was introduced
- Cliffhanger: Bea's ping revealed something about Simba's human contact

## Book 3 Continuity Requirements
- Simba is back and planning something bigger
- Barnaby is being framed for thefts he didn't commit
- The human collaborator (Mrs. Patel) is revealed
- Bea's ping ability is stronger but more painful
- The climax is on Sentinel Ridge
- The Meow-fia is temporarily dismantled
- Simba escapes (leaves room for future books)
