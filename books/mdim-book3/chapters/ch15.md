# Chapter 15: The Plan

My plan had six parts.

1. Maya jams the signal from the generator hut.
2. Henry and the dogs create a diversion on the lower ridge.
3. Feathered Frank warns us if cats are coming.
4. Barnaby and I climb to the transmitter.
5. I disable the transmitter.
6. Nobody gets eaten, arrested, or turned into a footnote.

Maya pointed at part six. "This part needs more detail."

"It is aspirational."

Barnaby studied the map. "The transmitter is on the old mast above the ridge path. There will be guards."

"Cats?"

"Cats, probably. Humans, possibly. Duke, almost certainly."

"Duke helped us at the boathouse," I said.

"He hesitated," Barnaby corrected. "That is not the same as joining."

Maya adjusted the detector. "I can get to the generator hut before midnight. But once I jam the signal, Simba will know."

"Good," I said. "Then he'll look at you instead of us."

Maya blinked. "That is not comforting."

"It wasn't meant to be."

Lulu raised her hand. "I can hide."

Everyone looked at her.

She added, "Or not hide. Depends on the mission."

Barnaby nodded slowly. "The small human may serve as a final emergency button."

"No buttons," I said.

"Understood. No buttons unless all other options have become soup."

Henry stamped once. "I will draw the guards down the south path."

"Are you sure?" I asked.

He chewed an apple thoughtfully. "I am large. Large things distract."

Feathered Frank puffed up. "I will spread the truth. Simba is a fraud. Simba is a fraud. Simba has terrible tail balance."

Barnaby approved. "Accurate propaganda."

Maya handed me a small silver device. "If you reach the transmitter, connect this to the main cable. It won't stop the signal forever, but it should scramble the command long enough for the police to arrive."

"What if I get it wrong?"

"Then the signal may get louder."

"Great."

Barnaby leaned against my leg. "You will not get it wrong."

"How do you know?"

"Because you are annoyingly thorough."

"That's not the same as good."

"It is when paired with my brilliance."

I folded the plan into my notebook and tucked it inside my coat.

At the top of Sentinel Ridge, the transmitter light blinked again.

Once.

Twice.

Then the whole ridge went dark for half a second.

Maya's detector screamed.

"Signal surge," she said.

Barnaby looked at me.

"Time to climb," I said.
