# Chapter 11: The Crumb & Kettle

The Crumb & Kettle was closed.

That was the first bad sign. Mrs. Patel never closed early unless something was wrong, on fire, or both.

The second bad sign was the cat in the window.

It was grey with one white paw. Marmalade.

She sat inside a small travel cage on the counter, watching us with round yellow eyes.

Mrs. Patel opened the door before I knocked.

"You shouldn't be here," she whispered.

"We know," I said.

Barnaby pushed past her legs. "Where is the prisoner?"

Mrs. Patel pointed to the cage.

Maya crouched beside it. "Marmalade?"

The cat meowed once.

Barnaby sniffed the cage lock. "Cheap. Cruel. Embarrassing."

Mrs. Patel twisted her apron. "He said if I opened the café cupboards during the blackout, Marmalade would come home."

"Who is he?" I asked.

"I don't know. A man's voice. He called from a blocked number."

Barnaby's ears flattened. "Human collaborator."

Maya checked the cage. "This lock can be picked."

Mrs. Patel shook her head. "No. If I let her go, he'll hurt her."

Barnaby looked at me.

I looked at Marmalade.

Marmalade looked at a sausage roll on the counter.

Cats. Even hostages have priorities.

"There has to be another way," I said.

Mrs. Patel's eyes filled again. "He knows about the Festival. He knows which cupboards have spare stock. He knows when the police will be busy with the fireworks."

Maya stood slowly. "This is bigger than Simba."

Barnaby's voice was low. "Simba built the cult. Humans are opening the doors."

I wrote it in my notebook.

NEW FACTS:
- Human voices helping Simba
- Glass Vaults relay nodes
- Mrs. Patel blackmailed
- Marmalade hostage
- Festival blackout
- Police distracted by fireworks

The café bell rang.

Everyone froze.

Mrs. Patel shoved the cage behind the counter and grabbed a tray. "Act normal."

Barnaby looked at me. "Define normal."

The door opened.

A man in a dark jacket came in. Not a customer. He did not look at the cakes. He looked at Mrs. Patel.

"Preparation?" he asked.

Mrs. Patel's hand trembled. "Yes."

"Midnight. Do not fail me."

He turned to leave.

Barnaby stepped into his path.

The man looked down. "Cute dog."

Barnaby stared up at him.

*'I am not cute. I am a tactical unit.'*

The man frowned and backed away. "Weird dog."

When the door closed, Mrs. Patel sagged.

Maya whispered, "Did you see his jacket?"

I had.

A tiny silver badge.

GV.

Glass Vaults.

Barnaby growled so quietly I felt it more than heard it.

"Simba is not the only villain," I said.

Barnaby looked toward the window.

"No," he said. "But he may be the only one foolish enough to give a speech."
