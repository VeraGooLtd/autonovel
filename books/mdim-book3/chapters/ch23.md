# Chapter 23: The Salami Solution

Just when we thought it was over, Simba ran.

He shot across the platform, leapt onto a lower branch, and bounced toward the festival path.

"After him!" I shouted.

Everyone moved.

Unfortunately, everyone moved in different directions.

The dogs ran downhill. Frank flew uphill. Henry turned left because a rabbit crossed the path. Lulu ran straight toward the biggest noise, which is how Lulu navigates life.

Simba reached the old storage shed below the ridge.

Inside was the 12kg Artisan Salami.

Book 1 salami.

The legendary salami.

The salami Barnaby had once described as "the only cured meat with a future".

Simba dragged it into the open with both paws.

Barnaby stopped dead.

"No," he whispered.

Simba grinned. "You fight with clues. I fight with meat."

He rolled the salami down the slope.

Every cat in Riverstone turned.

The food-raiding cats. The Meow-fia guards. The confused festival cats. Even the hedgehog looked interested, though I don't think hedgehogs eat salami.

The salami rolled.

The cats followed.

It was like watching a very greedy comet.

Barnaby stared after it. "That is a waste of excellent pork."

"Focus," I said.

"I am focusing on the tragedy."

Simba used the distraction to leap toward the road.

Police lights flashed below. Mummy's voice cut through the trees.

"Beatrice Chen! Stay exactly where you are!"

This was not helpful because I was already exactly where I was.

Duke sprinted after Simba. Barnaby ran beside him. I ran after Barnaby, which is how most dangerous things happen in my life.

The salami rolled into the festival crowd.

Cats piled after it.

Someone shouted, "Is this part of the show?"

Daddy shouted, "I think the salami is in charge!"

Mummy did not shout. That was worse.

She had the clipboard.
