# Chapter 7: The Alliance

By Saturday morning, the Canine Resistance had gathered behind the old sports pavilion.

This was less impressive than it sounds.

The Canine Resistance consisted of:
- Barnaby, who considered himself commander
- Henry the Horse, who was not a dog but had been invited because he was brave
- Three spaniels called Biscuit, Pip, and Another Pip
- A terrier named Colonel Socks, who wore one actual sock
- Feathered Frank, who was not a dog but said the pavilion had "better sightlines"
- Me, because nobody else could hear Barnaby properly
- Maya, because she had the detector
- Lulu, because nobody could stop her

Lulu arrived carrying a toy shovel and a jam sandwich.

"I am here for the mission," she announced.

Barnaby looked at the sandwich. "Is that strawberry?"

"Yes."

"Then you may stand near the front."

Maya set up the detector on an upturned bucket. "The signal is stronger. Sentinel Ridge is definitely active."

Henry the Horse lowered his huge head. "I can carry supplies."

"Supplies?" I asked.

He nudged a carrier bag with his nose. Inside were apples, two torches, a blanket, and a traffic cone.

"Why the traffic cone?"

"Safety."

Barnaby approved. "The horse has a point."

Feathered Frank fluttered onto Henry's back. "Pigeon scouts report cats near the ridge. Also a fox, but he says he is neutral."

"Can we trust a neutral fox?" I asked.

Frank puffed up. "He said he is neutral in exchange for crisps."

"Then no."

Barnaby climbed onto the bucket beside Maya's detector. The dogs sat. Henry stood. Lulu waved her sandwich.

*'Friends,'* Barnaby began. *'Allies. Persons of varying species and questionable loyalty.'*

"Oi," said Colonel Socks.

*'Today we face Simba, the Ginger Ninja, self-appointed ruler of Riverstone and owner of the worst moral judgement I have seen outside a magpie.'*

Feathered Frank nodded. "Magpies are dreadful."

*'Simba means to steal food from every cupboard in town. He will use fear, darkness, and probably a speech. Cats enjoy speeches.'*

Henry stamped one hoof. "We stop him."

"Yes," I said. "But carefully. If we rush in, he wins."

Barnaby glanced at me.

*'Beatrice is correct. We need a plan."*

Maya held up the detector. "I can jam the signal from below if I get close enough."

"I'll climb with Barnaby to the transmitter," I said.

Barnaby's ears flicked. "You will?"

"Yes."

He looked surprised, which made me feel strange. Like I had passed a test I didn't know I was taking.

Henry nudged me gently. "I can carry the heavy things."

"Frank can warn us if cats are coming," I said.

"I can scream very loudly," Frank offered.

"That's useful."

Lulu raised her hand. "I can press buttons."

"No," everyone said.

She frowned. "I can press the right buttons."

Barnaby considered this. "We shall keep that as an emergency option."

Maya drew a quick map in my notebook. Sentinel Ridge. The old transmitter. The festival path. The food stores. The police route Mummy had promised to organise.

It was not a perfect plan.

It was not even a very adult plan.

But it was ours.

Barnaby looked at me, then at the animals gathered around us.

*'As usual,'* he said, *'we are alone in our brilliance.'*
