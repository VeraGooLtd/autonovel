# Chapter 8: The Climb Begins

Sentinel Ridge looked much higher when we had to climb it.

From town, it is a green lump with a radio mast on top. From the bottom, it is a giant hill wearing trees and trying to be difficult.

Henry carried the apples. I carried the notebook. Maya carried the detector and two packets of biscuits "for glucose". Barnaby carried himself with the expression of a general who had not been issued enough troops.

Lulu carried a jam sandwich and a toy shovel.

"We should make a timetable," I said.

Barnaby snorted. "We have forty-seven hours."

"And Lulu."

"Ah. Then yes. A timetable."

Maya checked the detector. The red light pulsed every few seconds. "Signal is stronger. But it keeps jumping."

"Jumping how?"

"Like it is being bounced between little transmitters. Look."

She turned the screen. Red dots blinked across Riverstone: the park, the school, the bakery, the community hall, the café.

"Those are the theft places," I said.

Barnaby stared at the dots. "He seeded the town first. Clever. Irritating, but clever."

Henry stepped carefully over a root. "What is seeded?"

"Like seeds," Lulu said. "But for crime."

Nobody corrected her because she was right.

Halfway up the ridge, Feathered Frank swooped down and landed on Henry's back.

"Report," Barnaby said.

"Cats near the top. Many cats. One cat with a hat. One cat with a stick. One cat with both, which seems greedy."

"Human collaborators?" I asked.

Frank ruffled his feathers. "Two-leg shadows by the old transmitter hut. Blue coats. Not police."

Maya frowned. "Security uniforms?"

"Maybe," Frank said. "They smell like plastic and biscuits."

Barnaby's ears pricked. "Glass Vaults."

Maya went very still. "My parents' security firm?"

The detector gave a sharp beep.

We all stopped.

Ahead, hidden behind gorse, was a little wooden platform nailed between three trees. On it sat a black box with wires and a blinking red light.

Maya whispered, "Lookout post."

Lulu gasped. "A tree house."

"No," I said.

She was already moving.

"Lulu, wait."

She did not wait.

Lulu charged up the slope with her toy shovel raised like a knight's sword. Her foot slipped on wet leaves. She slid on her bottom straight into the platform supports.

There was a crack.

The platform tilted.

The black box slid off, bounced down the hill, and landed in a bush.

For a second, nobody spoke.

Then the bush sparked.

Feathered Frank screamed. The spaniels barked. Henry said, "Oh dear." Barnaby said, "Remarkable."

Lulu sat up, covered in leaves. "I found the bad box."

Maya ran to the bush and opened the detector. The red dots on the map vanished one by one.

"You broke the relay," she said.

"I helped."

Barnaby looked at Lulu with something almost like respect. "The small human has tactical value."

Lulu beamed. "I want another sandwich."

Then Maya knelt beside the broken box. "There's a label."

She wiped mud off the side.

GV SECURITY — FESTIVAL TEMPORARY NODE 4.

Maya's face went white. "This came from my parents' company."

Barnaby's voice dropped. "So the human collaborators are not random."

I looked up the ridge.

At the very top, behind the trees, a light blinked.

Once.

Twice.

Like an eye opening.
