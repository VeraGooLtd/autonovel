# Chapter 13: The Rescue

Lulu's rescue plan had three parts.

1. Create chaos.
2. Create more chaos.
3. Run when Barnaby says run.

It was not subtle. It was not safe. It was exactly what we needed.

We found Simba's camp in the old adventure playground at the edge of the park. Cats had tied blankets between the climbing ropes. A plastic bucket served as a throne. Two cats guarded a cage made from a football net.

Barnaby sat inside, looking furious and slightly dusty.

"You're alive," I whispered.

"I am detained. There is a difference."

Maya crouched behind a slide with her detector. "I can jam the local relay for thirty seconds."

"Can you free him?"

"Not without touching the net."

Lulu raised her hand. "I can touch the net."

"No," I said.

She was already gone.

She ran into the playground shouting, "I AM THE SAND QUEEN!"

Every cat turned.

Lulu grabbed the edge of Simba's blanket-throne and pulled.

The throne tipped.

Simba shot sideways into a sandpit.

For one glorious second, the entire Meow-fia forgot how to be criminals.

"Now!" Maya shouted.

Her detector gave a high whine. The football-net cage fizzed.

Barnaby pushed through.

Henry the Horse burst from behind the swings with apples tied to his halter like strange fruit. The cats scattered. Feathered Frank dive-bombed from above, screaming, "RETREAT! THE HORSE HAS ENTERED!"

Barnaby reached me, panting.

"Your timing is terrible," he said.

"Your kidnapping was worse."

"Fair."

We ran.

Behind us, Simba climbed out of the sandpit with sand in his whiskers.

"You cannot stop history!" he yowled.

Barnaby glanced back. "History needs a bath."

We reached the trees at the bottom of Sentinel Ridge. Maya collapsed against a trunk. Barnaby shook sand from his coat all over me.

"Disgusting," I said.

"Strategic exfoliation."

I checked my notebook. The countdown was down.

45:41:09

"We need the plan," I said.

Barnaby sat beside me. For once, he did not argue.

Maya opened her laptop. "I can jam the signal from the festival path if I get near the old generator hut."

"Henry and the dogs can distract the cats on the ridge," I said.

Barnaby looked at me. "And you?"

"I climb with you."

His ears flicked.

"You're sure?"

"No," I said. "But I'm doing it anyway."

Barnaby pressed his shoulder against my leg.

"Then we climb."
