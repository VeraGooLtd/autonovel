# Chapter 12: The Midpoint Reversal

The trap was obvious.

That did not stop Barnaby walking into it.

He said we needed to know where Simba's trial was happening. I said we needed Barnaby not captured. He said those were both valid points and then trotted into the alley behind the old bakery like a furry lawyer with a death wish.

I followed with Maya and Lulu.

"Bea," Maya whispered, "this is a trap."

"I know."

"You know?"

"I am nine. I know traps."

At the end of the alley, three cats sat on a wall. One had a paper crown. One had a stick. One had both, just like Frank had warned.

Barnaby stopped.

"Simba," he said.

From the shadows, Simba stepped out.

He looked exactly like a villain who had spent too long practising in a mirror. Ginger fur. Bright eyes. A black ribbon tied around his neck like a cape.

"Barnaby," Simba purred. "You came."

"I came to observe your collapse."

Simba flicked his tail. "You are charged with human loyalty, biscuit hoarding, and interfering with liberation."

Barnaby sniffed. "The biscuit charge is absurd. I share biscuits strategically."

Two large cats moved behind him.

I stepped forward. "Leave him alone."

Simba looked at me. "Ah. The girl who hears dogs."

My skin went cold.

Maya grabbed my sleeve.

Simba smiled. "You think you are secret. Animals talk, Bea Chen. Animals always talk."

Barnaby's voice came into my head, sharp with warning. *'Do not react.'*

Too late. I had reacted.

Simba's eyes gleamed. "You will be useful."

The two cats lunged.

Barnaby twisted, fast and low. For one second I thought he would escape.

Then a net dropped from above.

It caught him perfectly.

"Barnaby!"

He thrashed once, then went still. That was worse.

Simba padded closer. "The trial begins at midnight. You may watch, if you behave."

I wanted to run at him. I wanted to scream. I wanted to do something dramatic and stupid.

Barnaby looked at me through the net.

*'Choose both.'*

"What?"

*'You wanted to save me and stop the signal. Choose both.'*

Simba's cats dragged Barnaby into the dark.

Maya pulled me back behind a bin.

"Bea, breathe."

"I can't."

"You have to. We need a plan."

Lulu appeared beside us, holding her toy shovel. "I can press buttons."

I looked at her.

Then at Maya.

Then at the detector, where the countdown kept blinking.

46:03:21

46:03:20

46:03:19

Barnaby was right. I could not only save him. I could not only stop the signal.

I had to do both.
