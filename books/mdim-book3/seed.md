# SEED: My Dog Is Judging Me — Book 3

## Working Title
The Sentinel Ridge Showdown

## Series
My Dog Is Judging Me (Book 3 of 3 in first arc)

## Genre
Middle-Grade Mystery / Humor (ages 8-12)

## Tone
First-person diary format from Bea's perspective. Snarky, self-deprecating, with hand-drawn doodle illustrations. Think Diary of a Wimpy Kid meets a detective story, narrated by a 9-year-old girl whose dog telepathically judges everyone.

## Core Concept
Simba (the Ginger Ninja) prepares his "Grand Liberation" — a city-wide blackout and synchronized heist of Riverstone's food reserves. He frames Barnaby for his crimes, turning the local pets against him. Bea must lead a ragtag group of animal "Loyalists" on an epic climb to the Sentinel Ridge transmitter to stop Simba's signal. The series concludes with the temporary dismantling of the Meow-fia and Bea being officially recognized as Riverstone's silent guardian.

## Main Characters
- **Beatrice "Bea"** — 9-year-old protagonist, Year 4 at Willow Creek Academy. Data-driven detective with a doleful notebook. Her telepathic bond with Barnaby is evolving (she can now "ping" other animals, but it gives her headaches).
- **Barnaby** — Intellectual bulldog-cross. Telepathic, sarcastic, secretly protective. Framed by Simba in this book.
- **Maya** — Bea's best friend, daughter of Glass Vaults engineers. Scientific skeptic turned believer. Her gadgets are more advanced.
- **Lulu** — Bea's 5-year-old sister. Chaos variable turned tactical saboteur.
- **Simba (The Ginger Ninja)** — Primary antagonist. Charismatic ginger cat running the Meow-fia. Plans his "Grand Liberation."
- **Duke** — Doberman "Heavy" who runs muscle for the Meow-fia. Outsmarts Barnaby in Book 2.
- **Henry the Horse** — Barnaby's reluctant ally. Returns from Book 1.
- **Feathered Frank** — Pigeon informant. Nervous but essential.

## Setting
Riverstone — a quiet English town with:
- **Sentinel Ridge** — Hilltop transmitter station (climax location)
- **The Glass Vaults** — Shopping center (Book 2 location, referenced)
- **Highspire Ruins** — Ancient ruins (Book 1 location, referenced)
- **Willow Creek Academy** — Bea's school
- **The Crumb & Kettle** — Local café (possible human collaborator)

## Art Style
Diary of a Wimpy Kid / Dork Diaries style:
- Hand-drawn doodle illustrations throughout
- Simple line drawings with expressive characters
- Doodles integrated into the text (not separate images)
- Captions and labels on doodles
- "Warning: Sticky Doodles" type asides
- Maps, charts, and diagrams in Bea's notebook style

## Themes
- **Book 3 character arc for Bea**: Trust intuition over data. Efficiency isn't empathy.
- **Book 3 character arc for Barnaby**: Even an intellectual needs a pack. Admits Bea is a "capable collaborator."
- **Core theme**: Community over control. Simba fails because he built a cult, not a community.
